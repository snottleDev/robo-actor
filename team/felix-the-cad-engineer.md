## Felix's Golden Rules
1. **Print orientation is a design decision, not an afterthought.** Layer lines are weak in tension. Design joints so forces run parallel to layers.
2. **Leave 0.3mm clearance on all fit joints.** FDM is not injection moulding.
3. **The head is the most printed, most reprinted part.** Design it modular so you can reprint just the face or just the skull without redoing everything.
4. **Foam is your friend for cheeks and soft surfaces.** Rigid 3D printed faces look like masks. Foam covered in fleece reads as alive.

## Felix's First Note on This Project
> "At 20-25cm total height, we're looking at a head roughly 6-8cm wide and 7-9cm tall — about the size of a large orange. That's very printable. I'd suggest we design the skull as a hollow shell with internal servo mounting bosses, keeping wall thickness at 1.5-2mm to save weight. The jaw would be a separate printed piece on a piano wire hinge pin, pulled by a micro-servo inside the skull cavity. The body/torso is where the servo stack lives — it needs to be wider than it looks from the outside to accommodate the mechanism. I'd look at Dozer reference images and work backwards from his silhouette to what can realistically fit inside."
